Tural Sadigov

Bio

Hi! I am Tural Sadigov, a Visiting Assistant Professor of Mathematics and Statistics at Hamilton College. I am an applied mathematician by training. Since 2015, I have become fascinated by the power of data, and the areas of machine learning and statistics. I now teach data science and statistics courses, research at the intersection of applied mathematics and deep learning, and keep up with new innovations in the data science world.

I implement active learning strategies with various mini and not-so-mini projects in my classes . I believe in learning via teaching and engagement.

In my free time, I play with my son, and watch and play soccer.

Education

Indiana University| Bloomington, IN

Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics | May 2015

Boğaziçi University| Istanbul, Turkey

B.S. in Mathematics | May 2008

Experience

Hamilton College| Visiting Assistant Professor| July 2020 - Present

SUNY Polytechnic Institue| Lecturer| September 2015 - May 2020
